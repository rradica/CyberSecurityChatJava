# Email Security Policy

## Purpose
This policy defines the organization's requirements for handling email communications securely. It applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party partners.

## Reporting Suspicious Emails
All recipients must report suspicious emails immediately to the Security Operations Center (SOC) via the internal reporting tool or by forwarding to security@example.com.

Indicators of a suspicious email include:
- Unexpected attachments from known or unknown senders
- Requests for urgent payment or credential changes
- Mismatched sender display name and actual email address
- Links to unfamiliar domains

## Handling Attachments
Do not open attachments from unknown senders. If an attachment is expected but looks unusual, verify with the sender through a separate channel before opening.

## Supplier Communications
Invoices and payment-related communications from suppliers must follow the established verification process. Any change in payment details requires confirmation through the supplier's registered contact, not through the requesting email.

## Escalation
If in doubt, escalate to the SOC. Do not attempt to analyze potentially malicious content on your own workstation.
